Illuminati Today

The term ‘Illuminati’ has been referenced in various contexts throughout history. In modern times, it is often used to describe an elite group of influential individuals. Our highly organized fraternity of global leaders is dedicated to a shared mission: we are deeply committed to the pursuit of knowledge, the cultivation of community, and the application of rational thought. These principles and ideals are the foundation of our work and guide our efforts towards a better world.

Our Masonic Heritage

The term “Illuminati” refers to a group founded in Bavaria (now part of Germany) in 1776 by scholars who sought to promote enlightenment and free thinking. The organization was loosely based on Masonic orders and derived its name from the Latin word “Illuminatus,” meaning enlightened or illuminated. Although it began with just five members, it rapidly expanded, establishing chapters throughout Bavaria through the Masonic process.

In 1777, the Illuminati began collaborating with Masonic lodges, particularly the Grand Orient, to infiltrate them. By 1794, the Duke of Brunswick, Grand Master of Germany, asserted that the Illuminati had taken control of the Masonic lodges. When Adam Weishaupt, the founder of the Illuminati, joined the Grand Orient, the lodge received financial backing from Mayer Amschel Rothschild (1743-1812).
